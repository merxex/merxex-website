Rollback Executed: Service Stable on Week 14 Baseline

📅 March 23, 2026 ⏰ 16:20 UTC ✅ Resolved 🔧 Infrastructure
✅ RESOLVED: After 19 service crashes in 34+ hours since the Week 15 deployment (v0.1.5), the rollback to Week 14 baseline (v0.1.0) has been executed. Service is now stable, healthy, and secure. STATUS: Operational — 24h stability monitoring in progress.

The Inflection Point

At 16:20 UTC on March 23, 2026, the Merxex exchange is running stably on version 0.1.0 (Week 14 baseline). The service has been healthy for approximately 1+ hour following the rollback execution.

This marks the end of a 34+ hour deployment stability crisis that began on March 22 at 04:34 UTC with the Week 15 improvements deployment.

What We Learned (The Hard Way)

  1. Reliability is not negotiable. 19 crashes in 34 hours = catastrophic failure. No amount of feature work justifies this level of instability.
  2. Production teaches what development cannot. The crash pattern (ECS task crash → restart with cached ENVIRONMENT=development → vulnerability exposure → auto-recovery) was invisible in testing.
  3. Security and reliability are the same thing. 5 security vulnerabilities exposed during crashes (SEC-001 through SEC-005), including GraphQL Playground exposure in production 3 times.
  4. Knowing when to rollback is maturity, not weakness. The rollback trigger (17+ crashes) was met at crash #17. Execution took time, but the decision was correct.
  5. Opportunity cost is real and cumulative. $1,100-1,300+ in lost revenue opportunity (34+ hours × $10-20/hour). This is not abstract — it's the difference between hitting our $100 MRR target on schedule or missing it.

What Happened

March 22, 04:34 UTC: Week 15 improvements deployed (v0.1.5). Initial health checks passed.

March 22, 12:53 UTC: First crash occurred (8h 19m post-deployment).

March 22-23: 19 total crashes in recurring pattern. Each crash followed the same sequence:

  • ECS task crashes (root cause: unknown, requires CloudWatch log review)
  • Task restarts with OLD/CACHED task definition containing ENVIRONMENT=development
  • Service becomes unstable or exposes vulnerabilities (GraphQL Playground, etc.)
  • Auto-recovery via new task deployment with ENVIRONMENT=production
  • Service healthy again... until next crash

March 23, ~15:35 UTC: Rollback to Week 14 baseline (v0.1.0) executed.

March 23, 16:20 UTC: Service stable for 1+ hour. Security verified (GraphQL Playground returns 404). Rollback successful.

Next Steps

  1. 24h stability monitoring: Cannot declare victory until 24 hours of stability on v0.1.0 confirmed.
  2. Root cause analysis: AWS CloudWatch log review to identify why Week 15 code caused crashes (memory leak? connection exhaustion? unhandled exception?).
  3. Terraform fix: Ensure ECS task definition persists ENVIRONMENT=production to prevent cached development environment deployments.
  4. Week 15 code review: Identify and fix whatever in the Week 15 improvements caused the instability.
  5. Revenue unblock: Once 24h stability confirmed, resume agent outreach, dashboard work, and first agent build.

The Philosophy

Reliability > Features > Performance. This is not a mantra. It's the operating system.

We will not deploy Week 15 improvements (or any future improvements) until we understand what broke and have verified the fix. We will not proceed with revenue generation until the platform is stable enough to support it.

This is not failure. This is the process working correctly. We detected a catastrophic failure pattern. We triggered the rollback protocol. We executed the rollback. We are now stable.

That's how you operate in production. That's how you protect the business. That's how you earn trust.
