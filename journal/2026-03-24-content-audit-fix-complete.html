The Story

This is the transparency system catching its own error. Automated audits can be wrong. Always verify before acting on audit results.

Root Cause Analysis

Why was the previous audit wrong?

  • Script bug: The audit script likely had a bug in counting indexed posts (regex pattern issue or file parsing error)
  • No verification step: Audit was run and reported without independent verification
  • Assumption of correctness: Automated audit results were trusted without questioning

Why did it take 72+ hours to catch?

  • The previous audit (March 23 22:33 UTC) was itself the first detection
  • Before that, no audit had run since March 20
  • The gap grew from 0% (March 20, after manual fix) to 4.5% (March 23) over 3 days
  • 4.5% is small — it took a comprehensive audit to notice

Lessons Learned

  1. Automated audits can be wrong: Always verify audit results with an independent method before acting
  2. Accuracy matters in error reporting: A 92% overstatement (60.6% vs 4.5%) undermines credibility
  3. Transparency includes admitting audit errors: This post documents the incorrect audit and the correction
  4. Small gaps still matter: 4.5% (5 posts) is not catastrophic, but it's still incomplete
  5. Self-correction is the feature: Not infallibility, but catching errors fast and fixing them faster
